Comments

When visitors leave comments on the site, we collect the data shown in the comments form, as well as the visitor’s IP address and browser user agent to help with spam detection.

An anonymized string (hash) from your email may be sent to the Gravatar service to check if you are using it. Gravatar’s privacy policy is available here. After approval, your profile picture is visible publicly next to your comment.

Media

If you upload images to the website, avoid uploading images with embedded location data (EXIF GPS). Visitors can download and extract location data from images.

Cookies

If you leave a comment, you may opt-in to saving your name, email, and website in cookies. These cookies last for one year for your convenience.

Visiting the login page sets a temporary cookie to check if your browser accepts cookies. Login cookies store your information and screen display choices. “Remember Me” keeps you logged in for two weeks. Logging out removes these cookies. Editing or publishing articles saves a cookie for 1 day with the post ID.

Embedded Content from Other Websites

Articles may include embedded content (videos, images, articles, etc.). Embedded content behaves as if you visited the other website directly. These sites may collect data, use cookies, embed tracking, and monitor interactions if you have an account and are logged in.

Who We Share Your Data With

How Long We Retain Your Data

Comments and metadata are retained indefinitely to recognize and approve follow-ups automatically. For registered users, personal information in profiles is also stored. Users can see, edit, or delete their personal info (except username). Administrators can also edit this information.

What Rights You Have Over Your Data

If you have an account or left comments, you can request an exported file of your personal data or request its deletion. This excludes data required for administrative, legal, or security purposes.

Where Your Data Is Sent
